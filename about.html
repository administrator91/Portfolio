The Philosophy of

MonsoonInMySky

This is not merely a story; it is a confession of a soul steeped in one-sided devotion. It is the tale of a love so profound that it seeks nothing in return but the happiness of the beloved.

In this world, true love is often mistaken for possession. But here, the boy does not merely desire the girl; he reveres the very existence of her presence. To him, love is not something to claim — it is something to quietly feel, like a fragrance carried by the wind.

He often finds himself thinking, "Kaash tum mujhe pehle milti… kaash hum ek hi school mein padhte." He wishes he had been born a little earlier, or perhaps at the right time, so they could have shared the same classroom, sat on the same bench, walked through the same corridors.

If she loved her school days, he fell in love with the school building itself. He visits it not only to see her, but to feel her presence lingering in its silent walls.

To him, love is a mehek — a gentle scent that cannot be held, only experienced. He longs to walk behind her, if not beside her. He wants to breathe the same air where she breathed, believing that somewhere within it lingers the warmth of her existence.

He only wishes to be present — like an unseen umbrella during her storms, like silent shade on her brightest days. Her smile is his only prayer, her happiness his only desire.

Why "MonsoonInMySky"?

The name represents the eternal dance between Sky and Monsoon.

"You are my entire Sky, where every dream, every prayer, stops at you."

"A Sky that is completely covered by clouds of sadness. A Sky where the only rain that falls is the incessant downpour of your memories."
